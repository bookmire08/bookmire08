Cyclistic Case Study

GitHub Link

This case study is a hypothetical scenario based off real data.

We examined a year of data from January 1, 2025 through December 31, 2025 to identify key trends and rider traits. This task was necessary in order to better understand how to increase subscription conversions for Cyclistic casual members into annual members. Our assumptions are that the flexibility of the pricing plans with two casual rider options and one membership options have been successful in bringing us this far. My analysis will identify differences in how casual riders and Cyclistic members use the bicycles in order to provide suggestions that will assist in converting casual riders into annual subscribers.

The above data visualization contains a breakdown of the two available categories of ridership on a monthly basis for the year of 2025. We can see that the amount of overall rides increases greatly in the warmer months and that the proportion of casual rides increases along that same trend. Taking a closer look at the data reveals that from April through October the percentage of casual riders is upwards of 30% rising as high as 42.8% in July. This time period presents the biggest opportunity for impact from digital media campaigns.

Another important finding is that overall casual riders seem to prioritize the freedom to take longer rides by choosing day passes over memberships.

Long Ride Facts (Rides that lasted 45 minutes or longer)
 • Members took 59,502 long rides
 • Casual riders took 204,825 long rides
 • Electric bicycles were chosen for 75,162 long rides
 • Classic bicycles were chosen for 186,865 long rides

Short Ride Facts (Rides that lasted less than 45 minutes)
 • Members took 3,649,408 short rides
 • Casual riders took 1,946,833 short rides
 • Electric bicycles were chosen for 2,904,533 short rides
 • Classic bicycles were chosen for 2,548,771 short rides

Only 4.51% of all (casual and member) trips in 2024 lasted longer than 45 minutes in duration however 77.49% of riders that took trips longer than 45 minutes were casual riders. There is desire from Cyclistic members to go on bike rides longer than their specified 45 minute limit as 22.51% of the trips of 45 minutes or more were taken by members.

The data suggests that folks that take Cyclistic bicycles out for rides longer than 45 minutes have a medium to strong preference for classic bicycles, while folks that take Cyclistic bicycles out for rides shorter than 45 minutes have a weak to medium preference for electric bicycles. This means there is an opportunity to target the casual riders that prefer to take longer bike rides for conversion to members.

My first suggestion is to ramp up digital marketing campaigns from April through October.
 • The digital advertisement should be focused in areas of high ridership indicated by the heatmap above
 • The media for advertisement should be emails, app notifications and text messages
 • The goal of these campaigns should be increasing the total rides per month and the member’s percentage of rides for each month

My second suggestion is that we remove the 45 minute limit for members riding classic bicycles in favor of the same limits that casual day pass riders receive on either bicycle.
  • We would use this policy change as content for the suggested digital media marketing campaigns, informing casual riders that membership offers more freedom to ride how you like.
 • This policy change would allow memberships to appeal even more to current casual long riders while maintaining Cyclistic's commitment to providing inclusive accessibility accommodations.

My third and final suggestion is that folks that purchase a day pass for Cyclistic bicycles receive specific and targeted advertisements that provide discounts on upgrading to annual memberships based on the number of day passes purchased in the previous 52 weeks.
  • Someone who purchased multiple day passes would receive a text, email or app notification that they’ve got an exclusive offer to upgrade to an annual membership at a discounted rate based on the number and type of trips they’ve taken in the last year
 • This plan exactly aligns with the marketing goals of incentivizing casual riders to become members by providing unique benefits via digital media outreach
